About

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at Western University, advised by Prof. Grace Y. Yi and Prof. Boyu Wang.

My background spans mathematics, finance, statistics, and machine learning. My research develops statistically principled machine learning methods for learning from imperfect data, including noisy, crowdsourced, and heterogeneous supervision. I combine statistical theory with modern machine learning to study robust learning, uncertainty modeling, and reliable prediction.

More recently, I have become interested in multimodal medical AI and foundation models, particularly in settings involving uncertainty, imperfect supervision, and clinical reliability.

Available for postdoctoral positions starting August 2026.
